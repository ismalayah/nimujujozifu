Career and Professional Life

Matt Slays began singing and performing when he was quite young. When he was a teenager, he joined a boy band called Townsend in San Francisco Bay Area. He had to only give a few trials, after which Townsend selected him as a member. And, the group sang hip-pop style music.

His good looks and vocal qualities matched the expectations of the group well. He brought with him a little bit of experience in music, which helped the group significantly. In addition, he was an excellent lyricist and pianist, which the band was looking for. After he joined the band, he was chosen to be one of the 25 finalists for the “Making the Band” show. The show was a Lou Pearlman production, telecast on ABC television.

Likewise, while he was still in the university, he began working with Hilton Hotels in the sales department in February 2003. He worked with them for six long years. During this period, Slays also performed live to win an MTV song-writing competition.

Slays is now a composer, cinematographer, and editor. He has directed a number of web episodes and short films. He became popular with his music videos such as “Shit LA People Say”, and “Shit Fat Vs. Skinny Girls Say” in the year 2012, and “The Real Housewives of New Jersey Parody” in 2013. Likewise, his video titled “Michael Jackson Mash-up (Love Never Felt So Good / Man in the Mirror)” and “Fight Song Cover” in the year 2015 became immensely popular as well.
